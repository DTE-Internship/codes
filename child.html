Child Care

Child Care

D’Headstone Cheers is committed to every child, we provide every need for all children in our care who still reside with his/her parents or in an orphanage. There are very many essential things a child needs to grow and develop into a confident teenager, and these are what we deliver in top notch quality.

These are the current initiatives under the Child Care expression:

A. QUALITY EDUCATION

Education is the intellectual enlightenment from ignorance to knowledge. The goal of education is the advancement of knowledge and the dissemination of truth- John Fitzgerald Kennedy. Every child has the right to quality education, no child should be left roaming the streets for the purpose of daily bread. Quality education provides foundation for equity in the society. We at DHC understand the very importance of quality education and we try our best possible to empower children through our initiatives. Our key Strategies to providing or fostering quality education-

Back to school-

This initiative is committed to the provision of materials needed for quality education. Education should not just be available but must be enjoyable by every child, but this cannot be achieved except there are adequate and relevant teaching and learning materials like notebooks, text books and other reading materials, comfortable school bags, water bottles. Education can be enjoyable when all these materials are made available, but in the society were poverty is the norm for every 9 families in 10, academic sponsorship is needed to give every child the opportunity to attend a school and learn.

School kits-

Books, comfortable school bags, lunch boxes, water bottles, mathematical sets, pens, pencils, and even school uniforms are all important kits to achieve a smooth sail for a child in his/her academics. As much as school bags are overlooked, they are essential for the protection and carriage of books and other writing materials to and from school, they also affect the posture of a child negatively if not well managed and monitored, exposing the child to bone deformities, the child begins to slouch and this can also cause pain. D’Headstone cheers is sold out to giving children a good shot at quality education with quality tools and kits, making education fun and enjoyable.

Academic sponsorship-

he primary goal of sponsorship is to help provide children with their best chance for success. And since Education is the intellectual enlightenment from ignorance to knowledge, a number of parents lack the financial capability to provide this basic need for their children. D’Headstone Cheers! Is aimed at making sure to eradicate illiteracy as well as giving better opportunities to everybody. There are many ways a we can achieve these goals: donations (cash and equipment), scholarships, and so on. Regardless of the methodology, the aim is always to improve and promote universal access to education.

B. MEDICAL SERVICES (CHILD HEALTH)

Child health care is defined as the medical services that are provided by the medical health professionals to the children having diseases or infections. childs health includes physical, mental and social well- being. Most parents know the basics of keeping children healthy, like offering them healthy foods, making sure they get enough sleep and exercise and insuring their safety, but many parents cannot afford to do so.

Free medical checkups and support for children

Free medical checkups is one of the many strategies we adopt to touching lives at D’Headstone Cheers!. We visit communities and offer free medical services to the children in need of it. This is done by the Medical professionals Volunteering at the NGO. These professionals also proffer solutions to any problem found in the health of a child, free consultation and treatment for minor sicknesses. Free medical supplies are also given out.

C. PARTNERSHIPS WITH Orphanages, Hospices and Foundations.

D’Headstone Cheers! also partners with other NGOs to meet the need of other children who we could not reach through our very many platforms. We do this monetarily or by sending basic needs to the orphanages or hospices. We also sponsor campaigns for advocacy and volunteer to help in ways that will further improve the lives of the children. This is done so as to broaden the horizon of the NGO and not just restrict us to the children and other persons we can easily reach and administer help to.

D.HAPPY FEET! …Every child deserves to be Happy.

Shoe give away, Family Counselling and Community Bonding.

HAPPY FEET! is an initiative of D’Headstone CHEERS!, which is aimed at finding lasting solutions to Family disputes, Broken homes and Societal problems. And we plan on achieving this through strategic Education advocacy for children, from the individual level to the community level through giveaways and counselling (Public speeches) and Therapeutic help. We have a mandate to bring broken families back together in love, showing them the love of Christ at Christmas, and in turn making the society a better place.
There has been issues of disrupted families and distorted homes, which is detrimental to the quality of growth and development of children into responsible adulthood and in turn affect the society negatively. D’Headstone CHEERS! is committed to remodeling families in different communities one at a time, teaching and educating them on how family members should endeavor to maintain unity and harmony in their homes, resolve conflicts before they rare their ugly heads, the importance of education for the children, and also equally know that all problems in the family cannot be totally eradicated but they should be aware that the longer they are together the better for them. We would also implore the help of social service workers to provide assistance and advocacy to improve social and psychological functioning of children and their families. carrying out therapeutic techniques to help beef up the relationship between parents and their children be it a child, adolescent or youth. We will also continue to pay close attention to things that are indicative of poor attachment by the wards.
